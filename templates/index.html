      Popular Events in India

      Brahmotsavam(andhra pradesh)

      Tirumala set for Salakatla Brahmotsavam from todayBrahmotsavam in Andhra Pradesh is a major nine-day annual festival dedicated to Lord Srinivasa (Venkateswara) at the Tirumala Hills in Tirupati, initiated by Lord Brahma, featuring daily processions of the deity on various divine vehicles (Vahana Seva), attracting huge crowds for elaborate rituals, special darshans, and grand chariots (Rathotsavam).
      he primary Srivari Annual Brahmotsavam in Andhra Pradesh at the Tirumala Venkateswara Temple is scheduled for September 24 to October 2, 2025.

      Bathukamma festival (Telangana)

      Bathukamma is Telangana's vibrant nine-day floral festival during Navratri, celebrating nature, womanhood (Goddess Gauri), and the state's cultural identity with women creating conical flower arrangements, singing folk songs, and offering prayers for prosperity, culminating in Saddula Bathukamma (Dussehra) with large communal gatherings and Guinness World Records for floral displays and synchronized performances.
      Bathukamma, Telangana's vibrant floral festival, is a nine-day event typically held in September-October, coinciding with Navratri; in 2025, it ran from September 21st (Bhadrapada Amavasya) to September 30th.

      Navratri/Garba(gujarat)

      The traditional folk dances of Garba and Dandiya Raas are most famously and widely associated with the state of Gujarat, which is considered the heart and epicenter of these celebrations during the Navratri festival. However, their popularity has spread, and vibrant events are also major highlights in other states, particularly Maharashtra and Rajasthan.
      For the upcoming year, Navratri and Garba celebrations will generally occur from late September into early October, with specific dates shifting annually based on the lunar calendar; for example, Navratri in 2025 falls from September 22 to October 1.

      Ganesh Utsav (Maharashtra)

      Ganesh Utsav (Ganeshotsav) in Maharashtra is a 10-day Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha, marked by installing clay idols in homes and public pandals, daily prayers, and cultural programs, culminating in a grand procession and immersion (Ganpati Visarjan). Popularized by freedom fighter Bal Gangadhar Tilak in 1893 as a public event for social unity, it is now a state festival, with celebrations featuring traditional rituals, special foods like modak, and grand spectacles in cities like Mumbai and Pune.

      onam(kerala)

      Onam is Kerala's biggest harvest festival, celebrating the homecoming of the mythical King Mahabali and symbolizing prosperity and the bountiful harvest. The ten-day festival, celebrated in the Malayalam month of Chingam (August-September), features traditions like floral carpet decorations (Pookalam), grand feasts (Sadya), boat races (Vallam Kali), music, and dancing.
      Onam is a 10-day harvest festival in Kerala, with its main day, Thiruvonam, falling on September 5, 2025, and August 26, 2026

      Pushkar Camel Fair(Rajasthan)

      The Pushkar Camel Fair (Mela) is an annual five-day livestock and cultural festival held in Pushkar, Rajasthan, typically in October or November, coinciding with the Hindu month of Kartik. The 2025 fair dates are October 30 to November 5, 2025, and feature camel and livestock trading, cultural performances, and religious rituals, culminating on the full moon (Kartik Purnima).

      Bohag Bihu(Assam)

      Bohag Bihu, also known as Rongali Bihu, is Assam's most vibrant festival, marking the Assamese New Year and the arrival of spring (mid-April), celebrated with joyful music, traditional Bihu dances, feasting, and community gatherings for seven days, honoring agriculture, cattle, and family unity with rituals like bathing cows and preparing special dishes.
      Bohag Bihu, also known as Rongali Bihu, marks the Assamese New Year and the start of spring, typically celebrated for seven days starting around April 14th or 15th each year, aligning with the solar calendar..

      chhath puja(Jharkhand)

      Chhath Puja: Devotees Across Bihar Offer Prayers To Rising ...Chhath Puja is a major four-day festival of Bihar, dedicated to the Sun God and Chhathi Maiya, in which devotees observe a strict fast and offer prayers to the rising and setting sun on the banks of a river or water body, along with offering pure Prasad like Thekua, rice laddus and fruits, which symbolize nature, devotion and purit.
      Chhath Puja in Bihar in 2025 will begin on October 25 (Nahay-Khaay) and continue until October 28 (Arghya to the rising Sun), with Kharna on October 26 and Arghya to the setting Sun on October 27; it is a four-day festival dedicated to the Sun God and Chhathi Maiya.

      Goa Carnival(goa)

      All About The Goa CarnivalThe Goa Carnival is a vibrant, four-day festival of music, dance, and parades held annually before Lent, blending Portuguese heritage with Goan culture, featuring elaborate floats, costumed revelers led by "King Momo," and festivities in cities like Panaji, Margao, and Vasco, culminating in grand celebrations with dancing and feasting.
      The Goa Carnival dates vary yearly, typically held over four days before Lent; for 2026, expect it around February 13-16, while 2025 was March 1-4, and 2024 was February 10-13, featuring vibrant parades.

      Hampi Utsav(karnataka

      Hampi Utsav (or Vijaya Utsava) is a vibrant annual cultural festival in Hampi, Karnataka, celebrating the historic Vijayanagara Empire with grand performances, traditional music, dance (like Bharatanatyam), puppet shows, and a spectacular elephant procession (Jambu Savari), all set against the backdrop of illuminated ruins like Virupaksha and Vittala Temples, blending history with contemporary art.
      Hampi Utsav 2024:Entry Fee Dates, Venue, Major AttractionsHampi's dates relate to its history as the Vijayanagara capital (founded 1336, fell 1565), its recognition as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986, and the annual Hampi Utsav (often Jan-Mar), with recent dates like Feb 28 - Mar 2, 2025

      Jallikattu(Tamil Nadu)

      Jallikattu events in Tamil Nadu occur annually during the Pongal festival in January, with the season kicking off in places like Thachankurichi (Pudukkottai district) and Avaniyapuram (Madurai). It's a traditional bull-taming sport where participants try to hold onto the bull's hump as it runs through a crowd, symbolizing cultural heritage and the bond between farmers and cattle, and involves strict regulations for safety.

      Republic Day Parade (Delhi)

      The Republic Day Parade in Delhi is held annually on January 26th at Kartavya Path (formerly Rajpath) to commemorate India's Constitution coming into effect. The main event showcases India's military might and cultural diversity through marching contingents, military bands, and tableaux from various states. The parade starts from the Rashtrapati Bhavan and proceeds towards India Gate, concluding with the Beating Retreat ceremony. Tickets are available online via the Aamantran portal and at offline counters, with specific dates and times announced annually.

      Kumbh Mela(Uttar Pradesh)

      The Kumbh Mela is a massive Hindu pilgrimage festival in India, held every 12 years at the sacred sites of Prayagraj (Allahabad), Haridwar, Ujjain and Nashik, where millions of devotees take a holy dip in the rivers; It is based on astrological calculations (position of Jupiter and Sun) and is considered the largest religious gathering in the world. The fair is attended by saints, akharas and common people, and its main event begins with the Shahi Snan (Royal Dip), which is considered a symbol of faith and tradition

      Guru Nanak Jayanti(punjab)

      Guru Nanak Jayanti, or Gurpurab, is celebrated on the full moon day (Kartik Purnima) of the Kartik month, typically falling in October or November; in 2024, it was November 15, while in 2025, it will be November 5, and in 2026, it falls on November 24. The festival marks the birth anniversary of the first Sikh Guru, Guru Nanak Dev Ji.

      Minjar Mela (Himachal Pradesh)

      Minjar Mela is a week-long international fair in Chamba, Himachal Pradesh, held in July or August on the second Sunday of the Shravana month, celebrating agricultural abundance with cultural programs, processions, and folk performances. The name "Minjar" refers to maize flowers or silk tassels symbolizing prosperity, commemorating a 10th-century victory of the Chamba king and his triumphant return..

      Hemis Festival (Jammu & Kashmir)

      The Hemis Festival in Ladakh (formerly Jammu & Kashmir) is a two-day Buddhist celebration marking the birth anniversary of Guru Padmasambhava (Guru Rinpoche), the founder of Tantric Buddhism in Tibet, and symbolizes the victory of good over evil. Held at the Hemis Monastery, it features masked dances (Chham) by monks, traditional music (drums, cymbals, horns), and the unfurling of giant Buddhist paintings (thangkas), attracting large crowds of locals in traditional dress and tourists.

      Nongkrem Dance Festival (Meghalaya)

      The Nongkrem Dance Festival, or Shad Nongkrem, is a major five-day religious and cultural festival of the Khasi tribe in Meghalaya, India, held annually in autumn (usually November) at Smit, the headquarters of the Khyrim kingdom, near Shillong. The festival, led by the Syiem (chief) of Khyrim, includes rituals, a goat sacrifice (Pomblang), and traditional dances by young men and women to appease the goddess Ka Blei Synshar for a good harvest and prosperity, with women dancing in the inner circle and men in the outer, symbolizing the matrilineal society..

      Rajim Kumbh Mela(Chhattisgarh)

      The Rajim Kumbh Mela (also known as Rajim Kalpa or Rajim Maghi Punni Mela) is a major religious and cultural fair held on the banks of the Mahanadi River in the Gariaband district of Chhattisgarh, also known as the Prayagraj of Chhattisgarh; it is held from Magh Purnima to Mahashivratri, attracting saints, devotees and tourists from India and abroad, and is a confluence of faith, unity and culture, in which the holy bath holds special significance.

      Khajuraho Dance Festival(Madhya Pradesh)

      p>The Khajuraho Dance Festival is an annual, week-long event held in February, consistently scheduled from the 20th to the 26th each year, featuring classical Indian dance forms like Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Odissi, and Kuchipudi, set against the backdrop of the ancient Khajuraho temples in Madhya Pradesh. The 2025 event (the 51st edition) and the upcoming 2026 festival are both slated for these dates..

      Rath Yatra festival (Odisha)

      Rath Yatra in Odisha, particularly in Puri, is a massive annual Hindu festival where deities Lord Jagannath, his brother Balabhadra, and sister Subhadra travel in giant, ornately decorated wooden chariots from the Jagannath Temple to the Gundicha Temple, a journey of about 3 kilometers, during the Hindu month of Ashadha (June-July). Millions of devotees pull the chariots, which are rebuilt yearly, creating a spectacle of devotion, community, and cultural heritage, with the return journey called Bahuda Yatra.

      Durga Puja(Durga Puj)

      Durga Puja is a major Hindu festival, especially in West Bengal, celebrating the goddess Durga's victory over the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing good over evil. The 10-day festival, typically in September/October, involves worship, cultural events, and elaborate art, with the main celebrations spanning the last five days (Shashthi to Dashami).

      Hornbill Festival (Nagaland)

      The Hornbill Festival, known as the "Festival of Festivals," is Nagaland's premier 10-day annual cultural event (Dec 1-10) showcasing the rich diversity and heritage of its numerous Naga tribes through traditional music, dances, indigenous games, art, local cuisine, and handicrafts, held at Kisama Heritage Village near Kohima to promote unity and tourism..
